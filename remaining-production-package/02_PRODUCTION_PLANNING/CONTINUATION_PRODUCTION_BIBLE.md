# Govardhan Gatha — Continuation Production Bible (Bhaag 2–4)

## Continuity promise

भाग २ ले भाग १ को अन्तिम दृश्यबाट सीधै निरन्तरता लिन्छ: इन्द्रको सिंहासन काँपेको छ, आकाशीय कक्ष अँध्यारो र विद्युतीय छ, र इन्द्रको आत्मविश्वास छिट्टै अपमान र क्रोधमा बदलिन्छ। कृष्ण, यशोदा, नन्द बाबा, इन्द्र, व्रजको भीड, गाय, गोवर्धन, courtyard र throne room को visual identity Episode 1 references बाटै आउँछ।

- Dramatic movement: तर्कबाट सामूहिक निर्णय, निर्णयबाट उत्सव, उत्सवबाट चमत्कार, चमत्कारबाट इन्द्रको क्रोध।
- Main locations: courtyard/sabha, Govardhan पूजा मैदान, heaven throne room.
- Existing reusable references: Krishna, Nanda, Indra, courtyard, Govardhan/Vrindavan, crowd, cow, sweet spread, puja set, throne, sun rays, glow, storm.
- New reference needs: वृद्ध गोप hero variant; heavenly messenger; Govardhan पूजा मैदान/Annakut arrangement; विराट गिरिराज divine form.
- Ending continuity: Samvartaka storm clouds awaken for Bhaag 3.

## भाग ३ — जब प्रलय व्रज पर उतर आई

- Dramatic movement: आदेश, अनभिज्ञ शान्ति, विपत्ति, सामूहिक भय, कृष्णमाथि विश्वास, पर्वत उठ्ने चमत्कार, प्रेम/लाठी sequence.
- Main locations: throne room/storm domain, Vrindavan exterior, flooded village, Govardhan exterior, shelter beneath lifted Govardhan.
- Existing reusable references: Krishna, Indra, Nanda, Yashoda, villagers, cows, Vrindavan/Govardhan, lightning, wind.
- New reference needs: Samvartaka cloud entities; storm-damaged/flooded Vrindavan; lifted Govardhan underside shelter; wet costume/state variants; gopi hero; elderly gopa hero; villagers-with-lathis group; lifted mountain master state.
- Structural flag: supplied script places the lathi sequence after an `ENDING / CLIFFHANGER`. Preserve the text, but treat it as a continuation scene before Bhaag 4 unless the user chooses to split it into a separate part.
- Ending continuity: Krishna remains under Govardhan after seven days; Bhaag 4 begins as rain clears.

## भाग ४ — अहंकार का अंत, भक्ति की जीत

- Dramatic movement: safety returns, Vrindavan celebrates, Indra recognizes his error, descends without royal protection, asks forgiveness, and receives a lesson in power and humility.
- Main locations: under/around restored Govardhan, Vrindavan celebration, quiet throne room, clear-sky meeting ground.
- Existing reusable references: Krishna, Yashoda, Nanda, Indra, villagers, cows, Govardhan, throne room, sun rays.
- New reference needs: repentant Indra without crown/on foot; crown-in-hand prop/state; Indra bowing interaction; Krishna lifting Indra; post-storm clean Vrindavan celebration; aarti group.
- Ending: clean devotional closure. The supplied spoken engagement request remains script-locked, but should receive a separate user decision because it changes the ending from cinematic closure to direct audience address.

## Global visual rules

- Premium stylized 3D Indian mythological animation, family-safe, emotionally grounded.
- Preserve Episode 1 face, age, body proportions, jewelry, costume language, palette and lens grammar.
- Vrindavan: warm earth, cream, saffron, leaf green, golden sunlight.
- Heaven/storm: cool blue-violet, silver lightning, restrained gold.
- Red is used only for danger/anger accents.
- No modern objects, text, captions, logos, watermarks, horror imagery, random gods, duplicated leads, crowns on villagers, or costume drift.
- Spectacle must always express story consequence: worship becomes community action; pride becomes weather; protection becomes shelter; humility becomes physical descent.

## Audio rules

- Generated clips contain dialogue, restrained ambience and listed SFX only.
- No generated music/background score.
- Dialogue must be the exact Hindi from script lock.
- Use the approved custom Flow voice asset with `@Voice` in every speaking clip.
- If Hindi speech, lip sync or child speech fails, keep the visual take only and perform a separate consistent voice pass; report the failure rather than accepting drift.
